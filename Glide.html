Overview

Glide is aimed to empower students by providing them a tool to manage their time in university. It helps both a clueless freshman or a final year senior, to stay on top of lectures, labs, admin communication and all sorts of co-curriciulars happening in their campus.

The Prompt

One day over a regular phone conversation, my brother told me the sad story of how he missed a guest lecture by MIT Professor and great astrophysicist Walter Lewin. He is an aspiring astrophysicist, so I could understand his frustration when he missed the lecture. This made me wonder how did he miss it? And how many more students like him are missing out on important events in the universities?

Could it entirely be the fault of ignorant students? Or was it also because of lack in communication?

All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy.

According to a study done in 2015, extracurricular activities have positive effects on students by improving behaviour, better school performance, and increased involvement in class. Not only do these activities contribute immensely to the quailty of their school life but equip students with so much more like better social skills, reduced stress levels, improved concentration and positive mental state.

Time management a struggle

Those who practice time management behaviors are more clear about their role and perceive that they perform better, said a paper from RICE University. Whereas in reality 78% of students have a problem managaing time to which 80% of their instructors agreed.

Process & My Role

I worked from inception of this idea to creating hi-fidelity prototype. I did research, wireframing, information architecure and hi-idelity prototyping.
The project is under discussion to be built by IIT-Roorkee students.

Research

I conducted a simple online survey to validate whether this was really a problem students faced. Survey focused on understanding their information sources in the campus, digital consumption and their need to be notified of events.
Survey was sent to all students to which 78 students replied. To keep the sample size same across all the years, 20 random responses from each year were considered for research.
Final research synthesized of 40 students.

According to the report by Gartner, android caputured 81.7 % of the smartphone market. Another report by MoonTech Labs suggests that the this market share of android has only seen an upward rise since 2012. Amongst the next billion users only 45% resorted to desktop for internet consumtion.

Insights

• Different bodies relied on different channels to communicate information to the students.
• Senior students were better informed, as they were part of student bodies and thus had better network with other students.
• Word of mouth played a bigger role in getting the word around the campus than notice boards. So outgoing students missed less.
• Year 1 students missed the most events because of their limited peer network. Also year 1 students had most events as compared to other student like try-outs for student bodies, many orientations, numerous administration notices to follow up on.
• Meticulous students who actively checked e-mails and notice boards to maintained their schedules too missed less.
• There is not a single consolidated space in the campus where students can get every information of all the extra-curricular activities.

Why Notice Boards did not work?

Notice Boards are often crammed with all kinds of pamphelets, admin announcements, trial flyers and generic notices. They are disorderly and no attention is paid to make it visible to its targeted students. Students can not be held accountable for not being able to retrieve relevant information out of many paper pieces stacked on top of each other in no particular fashion, especially on busy days.

Why e-mails did not work?

Inboxes are often cluttered with unnecessary spam. On a slim chance when its not, e-mails are often read in a haste and if an e-mail isn’t course related, chances of dismissal is higher, students usually pay less attention to it. Even if some students paid attention, there has to be additional steps taken to complete the process. After finding a event of interest a student must make a calendar entry to get a timely reminder or write it on a piece of paper and place somewhere its visible so as to not forget about it.

Problem Statement

Information is scattered across many mediums which is hard to keep up with. Student are berefted of the chance at truly managing and maximizing their university time.

How might we consolidate every form of scattered communication at one place?

Solution Space

The goal was simple – to consolidate every form of communication in one place. An in-house app where notices from admin office, events organized by clubs, and other announcements are presented in an orderly fashion at students disposal.
I started with designing an android app, because as per the research majority of students had android phones.

First Sketches

After first few sketches it occurred to me that not just events but class schedule is equally important for a university student. The class schedule changes every semester! This was a no-brainer and I quickly integrated this idea, of having class schedule, in the designs because of the reasons -
1. Students typically used phone calendars or printed schedules. Having class schedule alongside event schedule really completed the picture for them. Inturn for the business, this meant students not going to other apps for schedule, resulting in more retention.
2. Students now really got a complete overview of their daily engagements in one place. Like never before, they are presented with a platform where they can thoughtfully organize their work and play much efficiently.
Two major categories of Glide now were– Events and Schedule.

Schedule

Landing page of the app is Schedule page. At one glance it gives overview of all the engagements of the day which are Lecture, Labs, Events (if any added) and self created Tasks. All these different engagements are denoted by different colors for easier scanability. Reminders can be set for one or all tasks as desired.

Events

All the events are present here in chronological order. Students are only shown events which are relevant for them, eg: event for year 1 football trials will not be shown to year 4 students.
Students can add desired events to their schedule to plan their days. They can also Favorite a student body of their choice and choose to get fututre event notifications from them.

Creating Events

Events can only be created by selected few with rights like Admin or student body heads. Along with usual content of the app they have access to exclusive functionality of adding an event. Creating a suitable flow for it was crucial.

Notices

These are administration notices that require no action by the student but are often very important. Notices are also contextual and will only be visible to relevant students. Publisher of the administration notice must mention appropriate student segment.
